## Common HVAC Problems in Redlands Homes

These are the issues we see most often in Redlands — recognizing them early can prevent far more expensive failures later.

**Condenser unit choked with dust and organic debris** — Redlands' mix of mature trees and dry valley air coats outdoor units with leaves, pollen, and fine dust that restrict airflow and force the compressor to overwork.

**Ductwork losses in unconditioned attic space** — Many older Redlands homes route ducts through attics that exceed 140°F in summer. Leaky joints in that environment mean a significant portion of your conditioned air never reaches your living space.

**Refrigerant charge dropping over time** — A slow leak doesn't produce an obvious failure. Instead, your system gradually loses cooling capacity while energy consumption climbs — a problem that often goes unnoticed until the next utility bill arrives.

**Uneven temperatures between upstairs and downstairs** — Multi-story Redlands homes often rely on a single system and single thermostat, which can't account for the natural tendency of heat to rise. Zoning modifications or damper adjustments can resolve this without replacing equipment.

**Burning smell when the furnace first kicks on in fall** — Dust accumulation on the heat exchanger during months of inactivity is normal and usually harmless. But if the smell persists beyond the first few cycles, it can indicate an electrical issue or overheating component that needs inspection.

**System running but not dehumidifying** — An oversized AC cools the air quickly but shuts off before completing a full dehumidification cycle, leaving the house cool but clammy. This is a sizing issue, not a mechanical failure.

**Frequent breaker trips when the AC starts** — Usually a sign of a failing compressor, a short in the wiring, or a capacitor that can no longer handle the startup surge. Resetting the breaker repeatedly risks electrical damage.

## FAQs

**How often should I have my HVAC system serviced in Redlands?**
Twice a year is the standard we recommend for every Redlands home — once in spring to prepare the cooling system and once in fall to check the heating side. The combination of extreme summer heat, valley dust, and cold winter mornings means both sides of your system work hard. Bi-annual maintenance catches wear before it turns into a breakdown.

**My home is historic — can you still install modern HVAC equipment?**
Absolutely. Many of the homes we work on in Redlands were built in the early 1900s. Modern equipment can be adapted to older homes, but it requires careful planning around existing ductwork, electrical capacity, and structural limitations. We evaluate the full picture before recommending a system, and we'll tell you upfront if ductwork modifications are needed to get proper performance.

**How do I know if I should repair or replace my AC system?**
The general rule: if the system is over 10–12 years old and the repair estimate exceeds half the cost of a new installation, replacement usually makes more financial sense. But age and cost aren't the only factors — reliability, efficiency ratings, and how long you plan to stay in the home all matter. We walk you through the numbers for both options so you can make a confident decision.

**Do you offer same-day HVAC service in Redlands?**
Yes. Most service calls placed before noon receive a same-day afternoon appointment. Emergency calls receive priority dispatching regardless of time. Seasonal demand can affect scheduling during peak summer months, but same-day service is our standard operating mode.

**Why is my AC running constantly but the house still feels warm?**
Several possible causes: low refrigerant reducing the system's cooling capacity, dirty evaporator coils blocking heat transfer, a duct leak losing conditioned air before it reaches your rooms, or an undersized system that was never adequate for your home's heat load. A professional diagnostic can pinpoint the exact issue — and in most cases, the fix is straightforward once the cause is identified.
