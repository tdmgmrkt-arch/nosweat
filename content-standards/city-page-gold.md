## Common HVAC Problems in Moreno Valley Homes

Recognizing these issues early can save you hundreds in emergency repair costs.

**Weak or uneven airflow** — Clogged filters, ductwork leaks, or a failing blower motor. Rooms farthest from the unit suffer most.

**AC blowing warm air** — Low refrigerant, a failed compressor, or a malfunctioning condenser. Requires professional diagnosis.

**System short-cycling** — Unit turns on and off every few minutes, straining the compressor and spiking energy costs.

**Unusually high energy bills** — If habits haven't changed but your bill jumped 20%+, system efficiency has degraded.

**Strange noises during operation** — Grinding, squealing, or banging indicate loose parts, worn bearings, or a failing motor.

**Thermostat inaccuracy** — Displayed temperature doesn't match the room, causing the system to over-run or under-run.

**Musty odors from vents** — Mold in the ductwork or a clogged condensate drain that needs immediate attention.

## FAQs

**How often should HVAC systems be serviced in Moreno Valley homes?**
Twice per year — once in spring before cooling season and once in fall before heating season. The extreme heat and dust in Moreno Valley make bi-annual tune-ups essential for catching small issues before they become expensive repairs.

**What size AC unit do most Moreno Valley homes need?**
It depends on square footage, insulation, window orientation, and ductwork. A 1,500 sq ft home typically needs a 3–3.5 ton system, but the only accurate method is a professional load calculation. Oversized units short-cycle and waste energy; undersized units can't keep up with Moreno Valley's summer heat.

**Can I get same-day AC repair in Moreno Valley?**
Yes. We offer same-day service for most AC and furnace repair calls in Moreno Valley. Calls placed before noon typically receive a same-afternoon appointment. Emergency service is also available after hours.

**When should I replace my HVAC system instead of repairing it in Moreno Valley?**
If the repair costs more than 50% of a new system's price and your equipment is over 10 years old, replacement is usually the smarter investment. Our technicians give honest assessments — we don't push replacements when a repair makes more financial sense.

**Why are Moreno Valley electric bills so high in summer?**
Summer bills spike because AC systems run nearly continuously during heat waves. If your bill seems disproportionately high, it could indicate a refrigerant leak, dirty coils, a failing compressor, or an aging system that's lost efficiency. A professional tune-up can identify the exact cause and often resolves the issue in a single visit.
